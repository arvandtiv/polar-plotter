# Round 5 reflection — LeWitt #38 (pegboard filled randomly → tonal mosaic)

**Ranking (best→worst):** 13, 14, 15, 16, 5, 7, 1, 3, 2, 4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12
**Note (#13):** *"perfect harmony and balance… 13, 14, 15 have a similar vibe — 13 for more
structured/straight line work, 14 for more organic/wild."*
**Scored:** 2026-06-30

## The signal — finer is better (for tonal mosaics)
Grid resolution maps almost perfectly onto rank:
- **Fine 10×10 (13,14,15,16) → top 4.** "Perfect harmony and balance."
- **Medium 8×8 (1–8) → the middle.**
- **Coarse 6×6 big tiles (9,10,11,12) → bottom 4.**

Many small random-toned cells read as a **harmonious, shimmering field**; few big tiles read
**blocky/crude**. The translation of #38 (random-filled grid → tonal mosaic) clearly *worked* as a
genre — the user engaged warmly — and the lever within it is **cell count: more, smaller cells win.**

## Also confirmed
- **Both crisp and soft hand work at fine grain.** #13 (crisp, j0) praised as "structured", #14
  (soft, j2) as "organic" — both top. So at fine resolution the line-character is a *flavour*, not
  a quality axis; keep offering both ends.
- A genre break from line-direction fields was welcome (re-confirms: variety across rounds matters).

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **For random tonal/mosaic fields, prefer fine grids** (many small cells); coarse tiles read crude.
   Could even push finer than 10×10 next time.
2. **Cell count is the dominant lever** for these fields — above line-character and contrast.
