# Round 2 reflection — LeWitt #16 (bands of lines in three directions, intersecting)

**Ranking (best→worst):** 10, 2, 3, 4, 5, 1, 6, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16, 7, 8, 9
**Scored:** 2026-06-30

## What won (top: 10, 2, 3)
- **#10** (fine lines @5 mm, **wide** gaps @50 mm) — the winner. Bands read as clean, almost
  solid **ribbons** crossing on lots of white. Fine *lines* but widely-spaced *bands*.
- **#2** (offset sets, phase [0,28,14]). Note: *"interesting to have the miss alignment of the
  boxes."* The three sets phase-shifted so their crossings never coincide → liveliness.
- **#3** (wide bands @70, big gaps @48, airy). Generous breathing room again.

## What lost (bottom: 7, 8, 9)
All three share a **large diagonal phase offset** (ph[2] = 60, 50, 52). Over-shifting the
diagonal set leaves it lopsided — concentrated to one side, big awkward empty triangles — and
the crossing reads accidental rather than woven. Note #8 was airy (gap 40) yet still sank: airy
didn't save it; the **excessive offset** did the damage.

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **Offset the sets — but moderately.** Mis-aligning the directional sets so crossings don't
   coincide reads as *interesting* (#2, explicit). Top picks used small/medium offsets (≈14–20);
   the bottom three used large offsets (≈50–60) and looked lopsided. Stagger, don't dislocate.
2. **Fine lines inside widely-gapped bands win.** #10/#3: tight in-band line spacing (5–8 mm) is
   *good* here because the **bands themselves are well separated** — they read as crisp ribbons
   on white. This **refines the round-1 "≤8 mm = mud" rule**: that rule is about *full-field*
   superimposition; inside separated bands, fine lines are an asset, not mud. The lever is
   **gap/breathing between bands**, not line spacing per se.
3. **Breathing room keeps winning** (consistent with R1): wide gaps > tight packing.
