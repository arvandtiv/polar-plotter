# Round 1 reflection — LeWitt #11 (four-part superimposed line directions)

**Ranking (best→worst):** 1, 8, 5, 6, 7, 2, 3, 4, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16
**Scored:** 2026-06-30

## What won (top quartile: 1, 8, 5, 6)
- **#1** (coarse 16 mm, rotating omission — each quadrant omits a different kind). The clear
  winner. Note: *"loved the breathing point and that you did not completely connect each
  quarterly space into each other."* → the four parts stayed **legible as four parts**, with
  white space between the line-fields. Coarsest spacing in the set.
- **#8** (orthogonals-emphasised, medium 11 mm) — calm rectilinear grid, quadrants distinct.
- **#5, #6** (rotation-shifted omission schemes, 11 mm) — varied direction per quadrant.

The common thread: **coarse-to-medium spacing that leaves air**, and **a different direction
scheme in each quadrant** so the structural division reads. The "wall divided into four parts"
must remain *visible as a division*, not dissolve into one uniform gray field.

## What lost (bottom: 14, 16, 15)
- **#14** (airy *uniform* omission — every quadrant identical): *"Boring as hell, stay away."*
  Airy wasn't enough — **uniformity across all four parts killed it.**
- **#16** (asymmetric weight, two dense @8 + two sparse @16): *"messy and boringly balanced."*
  My Klee "equilibrium-not-symmetry" attempt **backfired**: the dense quadrants read as mud,
  and a tidy 2-dense/2-sparse split read as *boring*, not lively.
- **#15** (fine 6 mm, all-different): too dense → muddy.
- The dense rotating ones (#4 @7, #3 @9) sat low too. **Over-density = mush.**

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **Keep the structural division legible; leave it breathing room.** Coarse spacing (≈12–16 mm)
   beat fine. Negative space and visible part-boundaries win (Klee #7, #5 line-density-as-tone —
   but don't over-fill).
2. **Vary the individual within the structural rule.** A *different* scheme per part reads alive;
   identical-everywhere reads dead/"boring" (Klee #11 individual-vs-structural).
3. **Density is tone, but past ~8 mm it turns to mud.** Avoid spacing ≤ 8 mm for superimposed
   fields (Klee #5/#9 weight).
4. **Don't chase tidy balance.** "Boringly balanced" is a real failure mode — a symmetric
   density split (2 dense + 2 sparse) is dull, and messy if the dense halves are over-packed.
   Liveliness came from **directional variety**, not from density contrast (refines Klee #8:
   asymmetry must create *tension*, not just an even two-and-two).
