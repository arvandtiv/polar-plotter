## Why it failed
#88 forces two constraints that fight the governing taste: a **grid** and **only four discrete
directions** per square. Quantizing a flow onto a 4-direction grid produced a **stiff, blocky
version of the round-11 stroke fields** — the free, continuous flow that made #86 a hit was lost.
It read as an inferior #86. Faithfully executed, this instruction simply doesn't suit the taste,
and dressing it up (flow-quantized directions) wasn't enough to rescue it.

## Lesson (→ LEARNINGS)
- **Don't quantize organic flow onto a grid / to four directions.** Free, continuous flow
  (strokeField) >> grid-quantized flow. When an instruction mandates a grid + discrete directions,
  expect it to read stiff; flag that to the user rather than over-engineer a rescue.
- Process note: this round was built autonomously before the "review each instruction together
  first" rule. Reinforces why that rule matters — review doability/fit WITH the user up front.
