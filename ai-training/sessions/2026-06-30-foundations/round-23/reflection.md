## Headline — the continuous-shading scribble is right; keep it OPEN/light
After three misses, the "shade" technique (one continuous coiling line shading an inverted-curve
arch as a volumetric form) **landed** ("great work"). The winners are unambiguous about the flavour:
- **#7 light single pass** (passes=1, low coils) — airy, open.
- **#5, #16, #12 loopy** (big loose loops, loopR 6–9) — bouncy, open, gestural.
- **#9 thin ribbon** (thin, light).
All the **dense / dark / multi-pass** ones (#2, #6, #10, #11, #3, #14…) were "too much — avoid."

## The rule — light, loopy, airy; density is "too much"
Same preference that's run through the whole session, now on the winning technique too: **keep it
OPEN and LIGHT.** Fewer coils, single/low passes, bigger looser loops, thinner ribbon, lots of
negative space. Heavy/dense/dark scribble = "too much" (also the paper-rip limit). The form should
read as a light gestural scribble, not a solid graphite mass.

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **`scribble` "shade" mode is a validated technique** — one continuous coiling line shading a form.
   Use it for the Scribbles genre and organic-form work.
2. **Keep scribble LIGHT + LOOPY + OPEN:** single/low `passes`, lower `coils`, larger `loopR`,
   thinner ribbon. Dense/dark/multi-pass = "too much" → avoid. (Openness preference, again.)
