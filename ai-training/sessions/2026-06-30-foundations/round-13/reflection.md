## Why: arcs-from-corners read SYMMETRIC even when hand-drawn
The user's exact words — *"the lines are still showing symmetric."* Four arc-families swung from
the four corners are a **globally symmetric construction** (mirror across both axes). The
per-line `jitter` roughens the *texture* but does not break the *structure* — the eye still reads
a symmetric geometric scheme. This is the governing taste asserting itself again: **organic/
asymmetric wins; symmetric geometry loses** (R8/#51), and — the new part — **jitter alone does
NOT rescue a symmetric global structure.** It's the same failure family as #88 (grid-quantized
flow): the *arrangement* is what reads geometric, not the line quality.

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **Jitter roughens texture, not structure.** A globally symmetric construction (arcs from
   corners/midpoints, radial families) still reads symmetric/geometric no matter how hand-drawn
   the individual strokes are. To satisfy the organic taste, break the **arrangement's** symmetry
   (irregular/asymmetric centres, uneven families), not just the line.
2. **"Arcs/circles from corners/midpoints" is a low-value genre** for this user (grid+arcs #130 =
   "not that valuable"). Treat the upcoming #138 (circles/arcs from side midpoints) with caution —
   it is the same family; flag the risk to the user before building.
