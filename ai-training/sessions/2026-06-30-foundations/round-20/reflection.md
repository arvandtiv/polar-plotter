The full-overlapping-circles / moire take on #138 was rejected outright. Despite per-line jitter, the

## The positive direction — WOBBLY or GRADIENT
The user named what they DO want in its place: **"wobbly or gradient-like feel."**

  round-11 stroke-field flow, the round-14 arc *scatter* — both hits). The line meanders; you don't
  read "circle" or "arc", you read organic texture/flow.
- **Gradient** = tonal density ramps (round 15 #142 "a success", the round-19 grays) — tone as the
  subject, not shape.

## Why round 14 (#138 arcs) won but round 20 (#138 circles) lost — same instruction
Round 14 broke the arc families into an asymmetric SCATTER → they dissolved into organic texture (no

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **Clean geometric SHAPES (full circles, tidy arcs, moire lattices) are rejected** — even
   hand-jittered. It's the legibility of the geometric form, not just symmetry, that reads cold.
2. **Replace geometric relations with a WOBBLY or GRADIENT feel** (user's explicit instruction):
   organic wandering lines that don't resolve into a nameable shape, or tonal density ramps.
3. Jitter roughens the LINE; it cannot save a design whose SUBJECT is a geometric shape. Choose an
   organic/tonal subject in the first place.
