## Headline — the new genre works, and DENSE webs win
The "located figures + hand-drawn location web" reading of #237 landed: the winner #13 is *"almost
perfect."* The decisive axis is **web density**: the rich construction webs won; the minimal /
light-web, figure-dominant designs were explicitly rejected as *"poor… not really representing
anything."* So in this genre, the LOCATION WEB is the subject — draw it richly.

## The reject rule — "avoid anything light"
Explicit: **avoid #1, #11, #15, #4** — all the light-web / minimal designs (few construction lines,
figures floating on open ground). The user's note overrides their raw list positions. A single
small trapezoid whispered onto the wall "doesn't represent anything." This is the governing
dense>sparse taste asserting itself hard in a new genre: **negative space is fine, but the mark
must be substantial.** (Refines the R6/R10 "fine+dense wins" — here it's web richness, not spacing.)

## What won — dense webs, corners included
- **#13 (winner): five figures, CORNER web, dense crossing lines.** I had flagged corner-radiating
  webs as possibly too symmetric/asterisk — WRONG. Dense construction lines to the four corners
  read as rich and structural, not stiff. The crossing long lines are an asset, not a symmetry dud.
- Top group: #13, #10 (six, medium web), #12 (mixed sizes), #3 (four, medium web), #8 (four,
  energetic hand). Common thread: **enough figures + enough web to fill the field.**
- The energetic hand (#8, jitter 5) placed well — living construction lines are welcome here.

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **Located-figure genre: the WEB is the subject — draw it richly.** Minimal/light figure-dominant
   layouts "look poor / don't represent anything" → avoid. More figures + richer webs win.
2. **Dense construction lines to the CORNERS are good, not too-symmetric.** Long crossing lines
   anchoring figures to the corners read as structural richness (contradicts my worry). Keep them.
3. For #238 (parallelogram) & #274 (six figures): build DENSE — more figures, richer anchor webs
   (higher anchorsPerFigure/vertsPerAnchor), lean energetic hand. Still density-capped for paper-rip,
   but push well above the light end. Drop the "minimal/sparse" designs entirely.
