## Two big results
1. **Range fixed the boredom.** Round 3 (all-straight, samey) got a flat 1→16 and "boring."
   Round 4 spanned straight→wild and got an *engaged, differentiated* ranking with enthusiasm
   ("love the feel"). **Confirmed: each round must span a perceptual range.**
2. **The sweet spot is straight → moderate hand-drawn; wild is "too broken."** Map of jitter to
   rank:
   - Top 10 (loved): the four **straight** (1,2,3,4 · j0), the four **light** (5,6,7,8 · j2.5–3),
     and the two **moderate** that read cleanest (9 · j5, 10 · j6).
   - Bottom 5 ("too broken"): the **wild** ones (13 · j8, 14 · j9, 15 · j8, 16 · j10) plus the
     tighter/uglier-seed moderates (11 · j5, 12 · j6).
   So hand-drawn **character is welcome**, but past ~j6 the line stops reading as a *natural hand*
   and starts reading as *broken/noisy*. Naturalness, not chaos.

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **Span a range every round** (the #3→#4 lesson, now confirmed by engagement).
2. **Hand-drawn jitter sweet spot ≈ 2–5 mm; cap ~6.** Straight (j0) is still loved when the round
   has range; wild (j ≥ 8) reads as "too broken" and loses naturality. Aim for a *careful human
   hand*, not a tremor.
3. **Seed matters at moderate jitter** — some seeds wander gracefully, some look noisy. Prefer
   gentle seeds; when in doubt, lower the amplitude rather than chase a seed.
