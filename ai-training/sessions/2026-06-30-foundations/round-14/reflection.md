## The headline — breaking the ARRANGEMENT rescued the genre
Round 13 (#130, symmetric arcs) was "not that valuable." The ONLY change here was breaking the
arrangement's symmetry (centreJitter / countJitter / radiusJitter) — same generator, same genre —
and it flipped to *"I like this way more than the original"* + *"very beautiful."* This is direct
confirmation of the R13 lesson: **for the organic taste, the symmetry of the ARRANGEMENT is what
matters, not the line quality.** A low-value genre can be rescued by making the structure
asymmetric, not by adding more hand-wobble.

## Transferable rules (→ LEARNINGS)
1. **Symmetry-breaking of the ARRANGEMENT can rescue a "low-value" geometric genre** — proven by
   #130→#138 (same generator, only the structure made asymmetric; verdict flipped to praise).
2. **On radial/arc families: push the OFFSET hard** (centres well off-axis) — gentle offset reads
   as still-symmetric and ranks low; strong/wild offset ranks high.
3. **Dense + asymmetric + coherent is the winner; but there's a coherence ceiling** — don't max
   every lever at once (8 families + jitter 6 + big reach + max count-spread = mud, "avoid").
   Keep ~4 families (side midpoints), density high-not-maximal, hand energetic ~4–5 (6 is
   uncertain again), and keep the concentric "echo" legible.
